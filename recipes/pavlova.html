Pavlova

Description

Pavlova is a delicious meringue dessert originating from either Australia or New Zealand1. Named after the Russian ballerina Anna Pavlova, this dessert is crisp on the outside but soft and slighty chewy on the inside. Be the talk of the table at any holiday meal by making this easy, yet show-stopping, treat.

Made up of only five ingredients, this is a great recipe for novice and expert bakers alike!

Ingredients

  • 3 Egg whites
  • 6oz (3/4 cup) Berry fine sugar
  • 1 tsp Cornstarch
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp Vinegar

Steps

  1. Prheat your oven to 300 degrees and line a tray with grease-proof paper
    • Do not use wax paper!
  2. Beat egg whites in a stand mixer on high until stiff
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together sugar and cornstarch
  4. Gently fold sugar-cornstarch mixture into your beaten egg whites
  5. Fold in the vanilla and vinegar
  6. Dollop out egg mixture and spread over the lined tray
  7. Bake for one hour or until golden around the edges
  8. After baking, immediately remove the paper and let the pavlova cool on a wire rack
  9. Top with whipped cream and your favorite fresh fruit and enjoy
